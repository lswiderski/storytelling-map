Paris, France

Paris, the City of Light, is one of the world's most visited capitals. Known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture, Paris offers countless attractions for visitors.

London, United Kingdom

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times. From royal palaces to modern skyscrapers, London blends the ancient with the contemporary.

Zooming into London reveals its famous landmarks. The city marker fades away as markers for specific attractions appear on the map.

üè∞ Tower of London

The Tower of London is a historic castle founded towards the end of 1066. It has served as a royal palace, prison, armory, and treasury. Today it houses the Crown Jewels and is one of London's most popular tourist attractions, protected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

üï∞Ô∏è Big Ben & Parliament

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster. The tower itself, officially called Elizabeth Tower since 2012, is an iconic symbol of London and the United Kingdom. The palace houses the UK Parliament.

üëë Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the London residence and administrative headquarters of the British monarch. The palace has 775 rooms and is one of the few working royal palaces in the world. The Changing of the Guard ceremony is a major tourist attraction.

Rome, Italy

Rome, the Eternal City, is a vast museum of history, art, and architecture. With nearly 3,000 years of globally influential culture, Rome has been a major human settlement since ancient times.

As we zoom into Rome, specific historical sites become visible while the city marker vanishes. Each location tells a story of Rome's incredible past.

üèüÔ∏è Colosseum

The Colosseum is an oval amphitheater built in 70-80 AD. It's the largest ancient amphitheater ever built and could hold 50,000 to 80,000 spectators. Despite damage from earthquakes and stone-robbers, it remains an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome.

‚õ™ Vatican City

Vatican City, an independent city-state enclaved within Rome, is the spiritual and administrative center of the Catholic Church. St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel attract millions of visitors annually to see Michelangelo's masterpieces.

The Trevi Fountain is the largest Baroque fountain in Rome and one of the most famous fountains in the world. Completed in 1762, legend says that throwing a coin into the fountain ensures your return to Rome. About 3,000 euros are thrown into it daily.
