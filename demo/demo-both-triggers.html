African Cities Overview

Welcome to a tour of major African cities. This demo uses both scroll and mouseover triggers, giving you complete freedom in how you navigate.

Try it: Scroll down normally to see locations change, OR hover your mouse over any section to instantly jump to that location on the map!

Cairo, Egypt

Cairo, Egypt's sprawling capital, is set on the Nile River. At its heart is Tahrir Square and the vast Egyptian Museum, a trove of antiquities including royal mummies and gilded King Tutankhamun artifacts.

The city is home to the ancient Giza pyramid complex and its Great Sphinx. With over 20 million residents, Cairo is one of Africa's largest urban areas.

Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos is a city in the Nigerian state of Lagos. The city, with its adjoining conurbation, is the most populous in Nigeria and on the African continent.

A major financial center for Africa, Lagos is also known for its vibrant music scene, particularly Afrobeats, and its busy markets. The city sprawls across islands and the mainland.

Nairobi, Kenya

Nairobi is Kenya's capital city. In addition to its urban core, the city has Nairobi National Park, a large game reserve known for breeding endangered black rhinos and home to giraffes, zebras and lions.

The city serves as a major hub for business and culture in East Africa. It's also a starting point for African safaris and home to numerous tech startups.

Johannesburg, South Africa

Johannesburg is a large city in Gauteng Province of South Africa. It was established in 1886 following the discovery of gold on what had been a farm.

The city is known as the economic hub of Africa and is one of the 50 largest urban areas in the world. Major attractions include the Apartheid Museum, Constitution Hill, and Gold Reef City.

Marrakech, Morocco

Marrakech is a former imperial city in western Morocco. It's a major economic center with mosques, palaces and gardens. The medina is a densely packed, walled medieval city dating to the Berber Empire.

The bustling Jemaa el-Fnaa square hosts snake charmers, musicians and food vendors. The city is known for its traditional souks (markets) and vibrant culture.

Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Addis Ababa is the capital of Ethiopia and the headquarters of the African Union. At an elevation of 2,355 meters, it's one of the highest capital cities in the world.

The city is known for its contrasts - modern buildings stand alongside traditional markets. The National Museum houses the fossilized skeleton of 'Lucy,' an early human ancestor discovered in Ethiopia.
