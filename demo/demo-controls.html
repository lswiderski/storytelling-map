European Capitals Overview

Welcome to a tour of major European capital cities. Use the control panel on the map to experiment with different interaction modes and settings.

Try switching between scroll and mouseover modes to see which one you prefer. Adjust the breakpoint position to change when sections trigger map updates.

London, United Kingdom

London is the capital and largest city of England and the United Kingdom. With a rich history spanning nearly two millennia, London has been a major settlement since Roman times.

Major landmarks include Big Ben, the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, and the British Museum. The city is a global hub for finance, culture, and education.

Paris, France

Paris, the capital of France, is one of the most iconic cities in the world. Known as the "City of Light," Paris has been a center of art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture for centuries.

The Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and Arc de Triomphe are just a few of the city's world-famous attractions.

Berlin, Germany

Berlin, Germany's capital, is renowned for its exceptional range of landmarks, vibrant cultural scene, and tumultuous 20th-century history.

The Brandenburg Gate, remnants of the Berlin Wall, the Reichstag building, and Museum Island are among the city's must-see attractions.

Rome, Italy

Rome, the capital of Italy, is a sprawling metropolis with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture on display.

Ancient ruins such as the Colosseum and Roman Forum, the Vatican City with St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel, and the Trevi Fountain make Rome one of the world's most visited cities.

Madrid, Spain

Madrid, Spain's central capital, is a city of elegant boulevards and expansive, manicured parks such as the Buen Retiro. It's renowned for its rich repositories of European art.

The Royal Palace, Prado Museum, and lively Plaza Mayor are central to Madrid's cultural identity. The city is also famous for its vibrant nightlife and tapas culture.

Amsterdam, Netherlands

Amsterdam is the Netherlands' capital, known for its artistic heritage, elaborate canal system, and narrow houses with gabled facades.

The Van Gogh Museum, Anne Frank House, and the historic canal ring (a UNESCO World Heritage site) are among the city's major attractions.
