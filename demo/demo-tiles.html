World Wonders Tour

Welcome to a tour of some of the world's most famous landmarks and wonders. Notice the dark, minimalist map style - perfect for highlighting colored markers and creating a modern, sleek aesthetic.

Different map tiles can dramatically change the look and feel of your storytelling experience. Dark themes work great for evening or space-themed stories.

Great Pyramid of Giza, Egypt

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only one to remain largely intact.

Built around 2560 BC over a 20-year period, the pyramid was constructed as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu. It stood as the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials. It was built along the historical northern borders of China to protect against invasions.

Stretching over 13,000 miles, the wall was built over many centuries by different dynasties. It is one of the most impressive architectural feats in human history.

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, the Taj Mahal combines elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles.

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru. It is situated on a mountain ridge 2,430 meters above sea level.

Built by the Inca emperor Pachacuti around 1450, it was abandoned a century later during the Spanish Conquest. It remained largely unknown until American historian Hiram Bingham brought it to international attention in 1911.

Colosseum, Italy

The Colosseum is an oval amphitheater in the center of Rome, Italy. Built of travertine limestone, tuff, and brick-faced concrete, it was the largest amphitheater ever built at the time.

Construction began under Emperor Vespasian in AD 72 and was completed in AD 80 under his successor Titus. The Colosseum could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators.
