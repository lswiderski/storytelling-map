Oslo - The Capital

Oslo is the capital and most populous city of Norway. Founded in the year 1040, Oslo is known for its green spaces, museums, and vibrant cultural scene.

Major attractions include the Viking Ship Museum, the Munch Museum featuring "The Scream," and the modern Oslo Opera House.

Bergen - Gateway to the Fjords

Bergen is a city on Norway's southwestern coast, surrounded by mountains and fjords. It's known for its colorful wooden houses along the old wharf, Bryggen.

As the gateway to the fjords of Norway, Bergen is a popular starting point for exploring the dramatic Norwegian landscape. The city has a rich maritime history dating back to the Hanseatic League.

Trondheim - Historical Heart

Trondheim was Norway's capital during the Viking Age and remains an important religious and cultural center. The stunning Nidaros Cathedral is Norway's national sanctuary.

Today, Trondheim is a vibrant university city with a thriving technology sector and a beautiful old town with colorful wooden buildings.

Stavanger - Oil Capital

Stavanger is Norway's fourth largest city and is often called the oil capital of Norway. The city combines modern business with charming old-town wooden houses.

Nearby attractions include the famous Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock), one of Norway's most spectacular cliff formations overlooking Lysefjord.
