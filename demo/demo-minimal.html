This demo shows the absolute minimum configuration needed to get started with the storytelling map library. Just define your markers and initialize!

This is the simplest possible setup for a storytelling map. It uses default settings and requires only a few lines of code to get up and running.

var markers = {
  place1: { lat: 40.7128, lon: -74.0060, zoom: 10 },
  place2: { lat: 34.0522, lon: -118.2437, zoom: 10 }
};

StoryMap({
  markers: markers
});

Getting Started

Welcome! This is a minimal storytelling map example with just three locations. As you scroll through this content, the map will automatically update to show the relevant location.

No complex configuration needed - just define your markers with latitude, longitude, and zoom level, then initialize StoryMap with those markers.

New York City

New York City is the most populous city in the United States. Known for its iconic skyline, diverse culture, and as a global hub for finance, arts, and media.

Famous landmarks include the Statue of Liberty, Empire State Building, Central Park, Times Square, and Broadway theaters.

Chicago

Chicago, on Lake Michigan in Illinois, is among the largest cities in the U.S. Famed for its bold architecture, it has a skyline punctuated by skyscrapers.

The city is known for deep-dish pizza, jazz music, the Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower), and its beautiful lakefront parks.

Los Angeles

Los Angeles is a sprawling Southern California city and the center of the nation's film and television industry. Near its iconic Hollywood sign, studios offer tours.

The city features beaches, mountains, and diverse neighborhoods. Santa Monica Pier, Venice Beach, and Griffith Observatory are popular attractions.

You've just experienced a storytelling map with minimal setup. The map automatically:

  • Updates as you scroll through content
  • Animates smoothly between locations
  • Uses default OpenStreetMap tiles
